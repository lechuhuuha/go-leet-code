A good next question for yourself is: after ReplaceRoot(node), why is heapifyDown(0) enough, and why do we not need shiftUp there?

After replacing the root, the new node is already at index 0, so it has no parent above it.
That means the only possible heap violation is with its children.
So I only need to restore order downward using heapifyDown(0).
shiftUp is used when inserting at the end, because that new node may violate heap order with its parent.

why must you swap with the smaller of the two children, not just the left one first?
When both children are smaller than the current node, I must swap with the smaller child to preserve the min-heap property in one step.
If I swap with the larger child first, the other child may still be smaller than the parent, so the heap remains invalid.
